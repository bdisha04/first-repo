D Gukesh

D. Gukesh, born on May 29, 2006, in Chennai, India, is a Grandmaster and the youngest-ever World Chess Champion. He is the second Indian to win the FIDE World Championship, following Viswanathan Anand, and also the youngest winner of the Candidates Tournament. His journey in chess began at the age of seven, and he quickly rose through the ranks, demonstrating exceptional talent and dedication. Gukesh was introduced to chess by his school coach, Mr. Bhaskar, at the age of seven. He later trained under Mr. Vijayanand and is currently part of the WestBridge Anand Chess Academy. He holds the record for being the youngest player to surpass a 2700 Elo rating, achieving 2750. In 2024, he became the youngest winner of the FIDE Candidates Tournament, and later that year, he defeated Ding Liren to become the youngest World Chess Champion at the age of 18. Gukesh's parents, Dr. Rajinikanth and Dr. Padma, are an ENT surgeon and a microbiologist, respectively. His family hails from Andhra Pradesh, but he was raised in Chennai. Gukesh's father has been instrumental in his career, even quitting his job to travel with him to tournaments. He has also received guidance from top-level coaches and is part of the WestBridge Anand Chess Academy.
