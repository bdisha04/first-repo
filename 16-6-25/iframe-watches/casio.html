Casio

Casio was established as Kashio Seisakujo in April 1946 by Tadao Kashio [ja] (1917-1993), an engineer specializing in fabrication technology.Casio is a Japanese multinational electronics manufacturing corporation headquartered in Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan. Its products include calculators, mobile phones, digital cameras, electronic musical instruments, and analogue and digital watches.

Key Features:

  • Digital displays with various timekeeping and other information, such as alarms, stopwatches, and calendars.
  • Many models also incorporate features like shock resistance, water resistance, and solar power (Tough Solar).
  • Beyond basic timekeeping, Casio offers advanced features like Bluetooth connectivity, heart rate monitoring, and even specialized functions like barometer, thermometer, and compass, particularly in their G-SHOCK line.
  • Casio watches often feature high-resolution digital displays with clear, easy-to-read information.
